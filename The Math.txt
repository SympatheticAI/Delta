## 2. Low-Order Wins (LOW) — Variational Theorem

Let (u) be a field defined on a domain (\mathcal{D}\subset\mathbb{R}^d).

[

}

[
\boxed{

f'(\cdot) > 0,\quad f''(\cdot) > 0

\sum_{n\ge 1}\alpha_n (-1)^n \nabla^{2n} u .

High-order derivatives enter multiplied by both (\alpha_n) and the global amplification factor (f'(\Omega)).

**Theorem (Low-Order Persistence).**
Assume:

1. (\alpha_n) is strictly increasing and unbounded,

\boxed{

and only finitely many low-order derivative modes persist with nonzero amplitude.

**Interpretation:** higher-order dynamical modes are exponentially suppressed by variational necessity, not by ad hoc truncation.
